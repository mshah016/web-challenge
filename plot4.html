Now clouds be scattered, my friends! This graph shows us that regardless of where they go, Santa and his reindeer are not free of the clouds unless they stay at the poles. Both north and south poles are fairly clear of the clouds; great for Santa and Twin Santa, but sucks for us. There points are scattered fairly uniformly around People-ville (any place inhabited by normal folk like you and me), making it very difficult to make significant distinctions. An every so slight pattern has emerged, however. It appears that the clouds accumulate at the latitude lines that are factors of 10. So at 20, 40, 50 degrees latitude, there is tiny clump that continues across all latitudes. You really have to squint to see it. So worry not, friends, if you were hoping to catch a glimpse of Santa this year without flying all the way up or down to the poles, make sure you are at a random degree latitude. I suggest 32 degrees.
