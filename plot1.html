This graph shows the temparature distribution among different latitudes. As the graph is a bell curve, it is safe to assume that the latitudes closest to the poles are the coldest and the latitudes located closer to the center are the warmest. This graph shows that the hottest points are located closest to the center of the globe, more commonly known as the equator, and that the coldest points are located at the edges, more commonly known as the north and south poles. So we already know that Santa Claus lives in the north pole. Does that mean his twin brother lives in the south pole? Equally as cold, so makes sense!
